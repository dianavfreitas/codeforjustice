Impact Of Fostering

The power of temporary homes is that fostering our animals is essential for finding them safe, loving homes outside of shelters. By opening your home to foster, you provide individualized care and support that significantly increases their chances of adoption. To put it into perspective, approximately 2.7 million animals are euthanized each year according to the (ASPCA) fostering can have an impact on rescuing more dogs and cats.

Sourcing Our Pets

Our foster pets come from local animal shelters, where they await their chance for a forever home. Owner surrenders also find refuge with us, as they face the possibility of going to the shelter if not taken in by a rescue.

Foster Care Requirments: Everything You Need to Know to Get Started

  • Age Requirement: 18 years or older.
  • Residency: Foster caregivers are typically required to be residents of the area served by the fostering organization. This allows for home visits, in-person training sessions, and easier coordination of fostering activities.
  • Living Situation: You must have stable housing and be able to provide a safe and suitable environment for animals.
  • You must be willing to foster our puppies or kittens for up to 8 weeks, sometimes longer if they need medical care.
  • If you own cats or dogs they must be up to date on vaccination.
  • You must have an area where you can keep our animals quarantined from yours.
  • If you rent your home, you must produce written consent from your landlord to have a pet.

Fosters provides rescue animals with temporary or long-term housing while waiting to move into their forever home, thus affording the pet a comfortable lifestyle that every dog and cat truly deserves and appreciates. Qualified foster care provider commitments include:

  • Feeding & boarding.
  • Behavioral training, exercise, and socialization.
  • Transporting to/from veterinarian appointments.
  • Assisting in finding the perfect family match for the pet.
